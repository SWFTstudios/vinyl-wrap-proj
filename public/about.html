The story

Building a life I
don't need a
vacation from.

This didn't start with motorcycles. It started with wanting a kind of freedom I saw other people build for themselves — and deciding to build my own version of it.

Chapter one

It started with
watching skaters.

Pro skaters had something I wanted: a life built entirely around a thing they loved.

They filmed it, traveled with it, and made a living being fully themselves. No cubicle, no permission needed. That idea got stuck in my head — not the skating specifically, but the freedom underneath it. A life you don't need to escape from on the weekends.

Where it began
Learning the craft
Chapter two

So I learned
to build things.

If freedom comes from making things people want, then I needed to become someone who makes things.

I taught myself web and app development — the skill of turning an idea into something real that works. That's still the foundation. But sitting behind a screen all day was only half of it. I wanted to build with my hands too, to see a transformation happen in physical space, not just on a monitor.

Chapter three

Then came
the bikes.

Motorcycles didn't replace the goal — they became the lifestyle around it.

Riding taught me presence. Wrenching taught me patience. And the 2002 Honda CBR600 F4i became the first real thing I could point to and say: I made this better with my own hands, and I documented every step of it. Fairings off, prep, vinyl on, reveal ride. A tired bike given a second life.

The F4i

The mission

Build a life around craft, discipline, creativity, and transformation.

Craft

Do the work with your hands and do it well. The finish should hold up to a close look — because someone will always take one.

Discipline

Early mornings, honest prep, no shortcuts. The boring, repeatable parts are what make the exciting parts possible.

Transformation

The whole point. Taking something tired and giving it a second life — a bike, a skill, or a life you'd rather not escape from.

The founder

This channel and this studio are the same project seen from two sides: a documentary about becoming someone I respect, and a service for riders who want their bike to feel like theirs. If you're here, you're part of chapter one. Thanks for riding along.
